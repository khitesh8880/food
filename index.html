A burger is a sandwich made of a cooked ground meat patty placed in a sliced bun. Burgers are often served with various toppings and condiments, and can include: cheese, lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, bacon, chilis, ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise. process of making burger

There are several steps involved in processing burgers, including:
Quality checks: Fresh ground beef is checked for quality and microbiological issues.
Processing: Ground beef is processed into hamburgers using a low-pressure forming machine. This method helps preserve the burger's taste, tenderness, and fragrance.
Customization: Burgers can be customized for weight, flavor, and with herbs and spices.
Packaging: Hamburgers are packaged, labeled, and stored in a refrigerating room until distribution.
Texture: The grind size of the meat and the orientation of the meat proteins determine the texture of the burger. The size and orientation of the fibers allow different amounts of air to be held inside the burger, which creates different levels of juiciness. Cooking: Burgers can be cooked on the barbecue or in a hot, non-stick frying pan with a little oil. Cook for 5-6 minutes on each side for medium and 8-9 minutes on each side for well done.
